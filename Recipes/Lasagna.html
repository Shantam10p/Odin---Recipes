Lasagna

Desription

Lasagna is a hearty and comforting Italian dish made up of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with rich, savory fillings. A traditional lasagna typically features layers of tender lasagna noodles, a meaty tomato-based sauce infused with garlic, onions, and herbs, and a creamy cheese filling made from ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. These layers are stacked together to form a flavorful and filling casserole that’s topped with a golden, bubbling layer of melted cheese. After baking, the lasagna becomes soft, with the sauce soaking into the noodles and the cheese creating a perfect, gooey texture. Each bite offers a delicious balance of savory meat, tangy tomato sauce, and creamy, melted cheese. Lasagna is a classic comfort food, perfect for family gatherings or cozy meals.

Ingridients

  • 1 lb meat
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (24 oz) jar marinara sauce
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (divided)
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley or 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 12 lasagna noodles (cooked and drained)
  • Extra mozzarella cheese for topping (optional)

Steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon. Drain excess fat.
  6. Stir in the marinara sauce, diced tomatoes (optional), oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  7. In a medium bowl, mix together ricotta cheese, egg, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  8. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, then cook the lasagna noodles until al dente. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  9. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  10. Lay 3-4 lasagna noodles over the sauce to cover the bottom.
  11. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of meat sauce.
  12. Repeat the layering process two more times, ending with a final layer of noodles and meat sauce.
  13. Top with the remaining 1 cup of mozzarella cheese.
  14. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25-30 minutes.
  15. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  16. Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
